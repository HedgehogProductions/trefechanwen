Birdwatching

Trefechan Wen is a good all year round base from which to plan your birdwatching expeditions. Sea Watching at Strumble Head, getting close to Puffins on Skomer Island or just sitting on the cliffs watching the Choughs. Local sightings are shared online so you might even tick a rarity. Make the most of your visit by tapping into local expertise.

“Memorable moments to savour — opening the back door (of the cottage) in the morning to the warming scent of the honeysuckle, a day walking on Ramsey Island seeing peregrine and chough — both with chicks”

Wildflowers

Visit St David’s Head in the spring to be greeted by a pink carpet of Thrift. Walk along the coast path and spot the orchids as you go. Enjoy the heady scent of honeysuckle on a summer evening while later in the summer the headlands turn purple as the heather flowers profusely.

Dolphins and Seals

The coves near Trefechan Wen are favourite spots for seals especially in the autumn when the pups are born. Harbour porpoises and dolphins are regularly seen from Strumble Head where Whales in Wales keep a regular watch.

“The cottage was cosy and well equipped. Lots to do and see… watching the sun setting at Strumble lighthouse and the surprise event of a pod of harbour porpoise swimming by. Magical!”

Wildlife Boat Trips

One of the best ways to see the wildlife that Pembrokeshire has to offer is by taking a boat trip. Take in the offshore islands of Ramsey and Skomer and get a different perspective of the local coastline. Head further out where and you might even spot a Whale.

“Venture Jet – a wet and wild trip around Ramsey Island – porpoises seals and seabirds abound.”
