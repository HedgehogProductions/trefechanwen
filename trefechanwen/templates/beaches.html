Pembrokeshire has over 50 beaches to choose from several of which hold awards for water quality and general management. You can find beaches with cafes and other facilities close by or you can escape to an unspoilt spot and have it to yourself. Trefechan Wen is well placed to explore the sand all year round.

“We also explored a couple of secret beaches — it is a real privilege to find you have an entire beach all to yourselves.”
Pembrokeshire has several beaches that are ideal for families and are patrolled by lifeguards in the summer months.

“Poppit sands and Whitesands were fab beaches, the boys loved going in the waves and rock pools. We caught so many crabs.”
Most of our canine guests enjoy visiting the beach — perhaps almost as much as their owners. Most of Pembrokeshire’s beaches are dog friendly but some of the more popular ones do have restrictions. “We and the dogs had a wonderful time exploring the area, especially the beach at Newport which made a perfect racetrack for two whippets.”
