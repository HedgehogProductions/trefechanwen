Pembrokeshire Coast Path

Pembrokeshire Coast Path. Enjoy 186 miles of breathtaking coastal scenery. Trefechan Wen is a good base from which to explore the Coast Path. OS maps OL35 and OL36 are useful. Local bus services, imaginatively named the Strumble Shuttle, Celtic Coaster and Puffin Shuttle are ideal for walkers.

Pembrokeshire National Park

Pembrokeshire National Park is Britain’s only coastal National Park and was established in 1952. The National Park website has a wealth of information including lots of walks that are not part of the Coast Path. We have printed a selection of these walks for our guests — including some ‘walks from the door’.

“Visited all the quiet corners along the coast from here down to Abereiddy we have come to love over many years of North Pembs holidays. The charm is that change has been minimal, long may it be so.”

The Daugleddau

The Daugleddau Estuary is the coming together of four rivers; East and West Cleddau, Carew and Creswell. It is a magical place good for walking and wildlife watching. It tends to be overlooked by visitors to Pembrokeshire so is often very peaceful and quiet. Discover Carew Castle, Lawrenny Quay or walk part of the Landsker Border Trail, all very different to the coastal scenery.

“Canaston Bridge/Slebech Woods, Minwear Woods provide some interesting views of the upper reaches of the Cleddau and its tidal range”

The Preseli Hills

The Preseli Hills provide another contrast to Pembrokeshire’s famous coast. This is a landscape of wild moorland, heath and grasslands. On a clear day the views are panoramic, taking in Snowdonia, the Gower and Ireland. Paths here are ancient — follow in our ancestors’ footsteps.
